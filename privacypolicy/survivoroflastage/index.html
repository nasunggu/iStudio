Data deletion requests

App: Survivor of Last Age  |  Developer: iStudio

If you want us to delete or de-identify cloud-side data associated with your use of the App, follow the steps below. This section is provided for users who arrive here from the Google Play data deletion URL field and summarizes what we can delete, what may remain, and typical retention behavior for the technologies used in this App.

Steps to request deletion

  1. Open the Survivor of Last Age app, go to Settings, then tap Data deletion request.
  2. When your device email app opens with a pre-filled message (including identifiers that help us locate your records), send the email to complete your request.

If the button is unavailable, you may email us directly at istudio1319@gmail.com using the subject line Survivor of Last Age - Data deletion request and include the same identifiers you would have sent from the App (for example your Firebase anonymous UID, your in-game nickname, and your device advertising ID (GAID on Android / IDFA on iOS) if you can copy it from the pre-filled message or device settings).

1. Information We Collect

We do not require you to create a traditional account using your real name, email address, or phone number to play the App. However, the App and its service providers may process certain information, including identifiers and technical data, that may be considered personal data or personal information under applicable law.

1.1 Information stored locally on your device

The App stores some information directly on your device in order to provide gameplay features and save your progress. This may include:

  • Game progress and gameplay data, such as level, experience, coins, weapon upgrades, stage unlocks, settings, and similar in-game data
  • Your chosen nickname
  • A locally generated identifier (for example, a GUID) that may be used for certain features if cloud sign-in is unavailable
  • Consent and privacy-related flags or tokens that the Google UMP SDK (or the operating system) may cache on your device as part of advertising consent flows

Deleting the App or clearing its local storage usually removes this locally stored information, subject to your device and operating system behavior.

1.2 Firebase Authentication (anonymous authentication)

The App may use Google Firebase Authentication with anonymous sign-in. This means the App may create and use an anonymous account identifier in order to support backend features without requiring traditional login credentials.

  • Anonymous user ID (UID)
  • Authentication/session tokens
  • Basic technical metadata required to provide the authentication service securely

We do not currently require email/password login or social login for normal gameplay.

1.3 Cloud Firestore

The App may use Google Cloud Firestore to store certain cloud-based gameplay data, such as leaderboard information.

Feature Examples of data Purpose
Leaderboards Anonymous UID or local ID, nickname, score, ranking-related values, timestamps To display, maintain, and update rankings and competitive game features
Nickname registry Normalized or hashed nickname references, display nickname, owner UID, timestamps To help prevent duplicate nicknames and maintain nickname integrity

Your nickname and leaderboard score may be visible to other players in the App. Please do not choose a nickname that includes your real name or other personally identifying information if you wish to remain anonymous.

1.4 Google Analytics for Firebase

The App may use Google Analytics for Firebase to understand how users interact with the App and to improve gameplay, balance, features, and overall user experience. Analytics data may include:

  • Gameplay events such as stage start, stage completion, game mode, map type, progression, and reward-related events (for example reward requests, rewards granted, and rewarded-ad completion)
  • Technical and app usage information such as app version, device type, operating system, and general interaction patterns
  • Advertising-related analytics events, including ad placement, ad engagement, and in some cases estimated ad revenue values and currency data associated with impressions or mediation reporting

Google’s platform may also collect certain automatically logged or aggregated technical data as described in Firebase/Google documentation; the exact categories can depend on SDK configuration and platform defaults.

1.5 Firebase Crashlytics

The App may use Firebase Crashlytics to detect, diagnose, and fix crashes and stability problems. Crash and diagnostics data may include:

  • Crash reports and stack traces
  • Device model, OS version, app version, and other technical diagnostics
  • Information about the circumstances leading up to an error or crash

1.6 Advertising, device identifiers, and ad consent (Google AdMob)

The App may display advertisements using Google AdMob and related advertising services. In connection with advertising, Google and its partners may process information such as:

  • Advertising identifiers such as GAID on Android or IDFA on iOS, where available and permitted
  • IP address
  • Device and app information
  • Ad request, impression, interaction, and performance data

Depending on your location, device settings, and the choices you make, ads may be personalized or non-personalized. We may share relevant information with Google and participating advertising partners to deliver, measure, and improve advertising.

When you start the App (and when required by your region or Google’s policies), we use Google’s User Messaging Platform (UMP) SDK to update consent information and, where required, to present a Google-hosted consent / privacy message (for example choices about personalized advertising, age-related requirements, or U.S. state “Do Not Sell or Share” / restricted data processing style options, depending on how your message is configured in the AdMob / Privacy & messaging console).

UMP may store consent outcomes and related technical data on your device and communicate them to Google’s advertising services so that ad requests respect your choices. The exact wording and options come from the message you see, not from a separate custom in-app form built by us for that purpose.

Where applicable law requires additional mechanisms, we may configure or update UMP messages and related settings in line with Google’s guidance for the EEA, UK, Switzerland, certain U.S. states, and other regions.

The App is not intended to be directed primarily to children under 13 and is not presented as a child-directed app for purposes such as COPPA-style treatment in advertising settings. The App contains combat/shooting content. The recommended minimum age and content descriptors for the App are those shown on the App’s Google Play and Apple App Store listings; if those listings differ from this Policy, the store listings control for age-rating purposes.

1.7 Information we do not intentionally collect directly

We do not ask you to enter your real name, postal address, or phone number in order to play the App. We do not use the App as a dedicated feature to collect your precise GPS location.
